Europa League

The UEFA Europa League is an annual football club competition organized by the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA) for eligible European football clubs. It is the second-tier competition of European club football, ranking below the UEFA Champions League.

History

The competition was first introduced in 1971 as the UEFA Cup. In 2009, the tournament was rebranded as the UEFA Europa League, incorporating the UEFA Intertoto Cup into its structure and adding a group stage.

Format

The competition consists of several stages, including qualifying rounds, a group stage, knockout rounds, and a final. Teams qualify for the competition based on their performance in their national leagues and cup competitions. The group stage is followed by a knockout phase, culminating in the final held at a neutral venue.
