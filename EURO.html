UEFA European Championship

The UEFA European Championship, commonly known as the Euros, is the primary association football competition contested by the senior men's national teams of the members of the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA). It is held every four years.

History

The competition was first held in 1960 as the European Nations' Cup. It was initially a four-team tournament and has since expanded to include 24 teams. The tournament's format has changed over the years, with the current format including a group stage followed by knockout rounds.

Format

The competition involves a qualification phase where national teams compete for a place in the finals. The final tournament is held in a selected host country or countries and consists of a group stage followed by knockout rounds, culminating in the final.

Recent Champions

  • 2020: Italy

  • 2016: Portugal

  • 2012: Spain
