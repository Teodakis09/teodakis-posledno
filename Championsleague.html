Champions League

The UEFA Champions League is an annual club football competition organized by the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA) and contested by top-division European clubs. It is one of the most prestigious tournaments in the world and the most prestigious club competition in European football, played by the national league champions (and, for some nations, one or more runners-up) of the strongest UEFA national associations.

History

The competition was first introduced in 1955 as the European Champion Clubs' Cup, commonly known as the European Cup. It initially consisted of a straight knockout tournament open only to the champion club of each country. In 1992, the tournament was rebranded and expanded to include a round-robin group stage and more teams.

Format

The competition consists of several stages, including a group stage, knockout rounds, and a final. Teams qualify for the competition based on their performance in their national leagues and cup competitions. The group stage is followed by a knockout phase, culminating in the final held at a neutral venue.
