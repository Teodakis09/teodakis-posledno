UEFA Super Cup

The UEFA Super Cup is an annual football match organized by the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA) and contested by the reigning champions of the two main European club competitions, the UEFA Champions League and the UEFA Europa League (since 2021, the UEFA Europa Conference League winner also participates in a play-off).

History

The competition was first held in 1972 as a two-legged tie and officially recognized by UEFA in 1973. Initially, it was contested between the winners of the European Cup/Champions League and the Cup Winners' Cup. After the discontinuation of the Cup Winners' Cup in 1999, the UEFA Cup/Europa League winners have competed for the trophy.

Format

The match is typically played in August, before the start of the domestic league seasons, at a neutral venue. It serves as a curtain-raiser for the new European football season.

Recent Champions

  • 2023: Manchester City

  • 2022: Real Madrid

  • 2021: Chelsea

  • 2020: Bayern Munich

  • 2019: Liverpool
